Welcome to Amsterdam









The capital of the Netherlands is known for its artistic and cultural heritage, with its many canal systems and tourists using bicycle power to get around, it’s a city full of character.


Amsterdam truly has something for everyone, which is what makes it one of the most popular tourist destinations in Europe. Whether it’s shopping, art, sightseeing, food and drink, or a ride in a canal boat that you’re after – you’ll certainly find it in Amsterdam.

1. Rijksmuseum

Situated on the famous Museumplein in the centre of the city, you’ll find a range of art and artefacts spanning over 800 years of Dutch history, as well as pieces from all over the world. Whether you’d like to see art by the Dutch greats like Van Gogh or Rembrandt, or just peruse their expansive collection, or just to marvel at the beautiful 1876 building – it’s the place to go when visiting Amsterdam.

2. Stedelijk Museum

The Stedelijk is Amsterdam’s museum of modern art – unlike the other two art museums and galleries on the Museumplein, the Stedelijk is dedicated to the last 150 years of art, textiles and sculpture. You’ll also find other interesting contemporary design pieces here, like furniture, jewellery and ceramics.

3. The Van Gogh Museum

Also found on Amsterdam’s Museumplein, the Van Gogh museum houses the largest collection of Van Gogh’s work anywhere in the world – when visiting you’ll be able to see over 200 of his paintings, all in one place. As well as paintings, they also have a large collection of his drawings and letters that you won’t find anywhere else.

4. Anne Frank House

The Anne Frank House – where she and her family hid for over two years from Nazi persecution during World War II – has now been turned into a museum. You can visit Prinsengracht 263 to see the place that they hid until their home was compromised in 1944. Anne’s original diary is still on display within the museum, as well as other notebooks and personal belongings that were left behind – it is a truly eye-opening glimpse into the life of Jews during the war.

5. IJ Hallen

If it’s shopping you’re looking for, IJ Hallen is the largest flea market in Amsterdam. Here you’ll find stalls selling everything from homemade items to vintage clothing – with hundreds of stalls to choose from, you’re sure to spot something that catches your eye!

6. De 9 Straatjes

De 9 Straatjes (which translates to The 9 Streets) are a collection of narrow streets where tradition meets modernity – these cosy streets are home to an array of different shops, café’s, boutiques and galleries.

7. Amsterdamse Bos

If you’re more at home in nature than the city, you could visit the Amsterdamse Bos, a picturesque forest located at the edge of the city. You can take a walk in the fresh air, and enjoy the scenery and a picnic, away from the hustle and bustle of the city.

8. Vondelplatz

If you’d like a break from being inside the many museums of the Museumplein, just a short walk away is another vibrant park called the Vondelplatz, a popular location for tourists and locals alike! Free shows and concerts are often held at the open-air pavilion – or you could simply cycle or stroll through the gardens!

9. Take a ride on a canal boat

The canals are a prominent feature of the city, and something it’s commonly known for. Why not explore the city by boat instead?

10. Ride a bike through the city

Amsterdam is characterised by it’s bicycles, many tourists and locals choosing cycling as their method of travel between the many different tourist destinations across the city. Why not hire one and see where it takes you!
