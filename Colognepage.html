City History

The Dom, Cologne Cathedral

Wherever you stand in Cologne, the cathedral can be seen towering above rooftops and chimneys, 157m high at its tallest point.
This gothic cathedral features many colourful stain glass windows,
and was one of the only parts of the city which survived bombing in the second world war, it was extensively damaged, and became the centre of the cities rebuild.

Timeride

Timeride gives you a peak into Cologne at the beginning of the 20th Century.
It is an an immersive Virtual reality experience, taking you on a tram tour of the old city, before both world wars.
You can visit some of these areas by foot, to see how different the city is today.

Museums

Imhoff Chocolate Museum

Next on the tour, head to the Imhoff Chocolate Museum.
Learn about the process of chocolate making, from bean to bar, and taste some for yourself. If you are feeling hungry, the cafe serves delicious pancakes coated in fruit and chocolate.

Olympic Museum

For the sports fanatics, the Olympic museum is right next door.
There are lots of Olympic style games and tests you can attempt, bringing out the competitive streak in everyone, it even has a rooftop football pitch.

The Views

The Traingle

After you have seen the sights of Cologne up close, the Triangle is the perfect way to round off the day.
If you aren’t afraid of heights that is. Take the lift up to the 29th floor, and take in the panoramic views of the city, the cathedral, and the river Rhine.

Cable Car

It’s the end of a long, sunny day.
Your feet have taken you across the city.
Head to the cable car, and take in sights of Cologne as you traverse across the Rhine.
It's a slightly bumpy ride, so not one for those not afraid of heights, but the views are incredible.

Sunset Drinks

It’s the end of a long, sunny day.
Your feet have taken you across the city. Head to the cable car, and watch the sun go down over the Rhine.
It's a slightly bumpy ride at not one for those not afraid of heights, but the views are worth it.
